### 🧪 Frameworks &amp; methods

- **[falsifiable-targets](https://github.com/crisprking/falsifiable-targets)** ![★](https://img.shields.io/github/stars/crisprking/falsifiable-targets?style=flat-square&label=%E2%98%85&labelColor=1b1f23&color=e3b341) — content-addressed audit engine for drug-target direction-of-effect (inhibit vs. activate), built on Open Targets colocalization and benchmarked against approved drug–target pairs. `83% acc · 50% cov`, failure modes documented not hidden.
- **[nav17-asymmetry](https://github.com/crisprking/nav17-asymmetry)** ![★](https://img.shields.io/github/stars/crisprking/nav17-asymmetry?style=flat-square&label=%E2%98%85&labelColor=1b1f23&color=e3b341) — why the best genetically-validated pain target (Nav1.7 / SCN9A) keeps failing in the clinic: a genetic–pharmacological asymmetry, worked through single-cell, a homeostatic-compensation model, and human genetics.
- **[esm-trust](https://github.com/crisprking/esm-trust)** ![★](https://img.shields.io/github/stars/crisprking/esm-trust?style=flat-square&label=%E2%98%85&labelColor=1b1f23&color=e3b341) — when *can* you trust ESM-C zero-shot variant-effect rankings? A reproducible benchmark showing a model's self-consistency does **not** predict its accuracy, plus a calibration tool.

### 🎯 Target-discovery pipelines

- **[t1d-celltype-of-action](https://github.com/crisprking/t1d-celltype-of-action)** ![★](https://img.shields.io/github/stars/crisprking/t1d-celltype-of-action?style=flat-square&label=%E2%98%85&labelColor=1b1f23&color=e3b341) — maps type-1-diabetes GWAS loci to the pancreatic cell types they act in, τ-specificity over the HPAP atlas. `21 calls · 145 loci`.
- **[madurella-target-discovery](https://github.com/crisprking/madurella-target-discovery)** ![★](https://img.shields.io/github/stars/crisprking/madurella-target-discovery?style=flat-square&label=%E2%98%85&labelColor=1b1f23&color=e3b341) — ChEMBL-driven target pipeline on a neglected fungal pathogen nobody had mapped — and it catches its own artifact: the "top" gene was 384 duplicate HDAC4 records. `10,707 → 7`.
- **[cruzain-in-silico-pipeline](https://github.com/crisprking/cruzain-in-silico-pipeline)** ![★](https://img.shields.io/github/stars/crisprking/cruzain-in-silico-pipeline?style=flat-square&label=%E2%98%85&labelColor=1b1f23&color=e3b341) — 8-stage open drug-discovery pipeline for Chagas (T. cruzi cruzain): three scoring tracks fused to consensus, selectivity counter-screen vs. human cathepsins, ADMET. Runs on a free Kaggle T4.
- **[enpp1](https://github.com/crisprking/enpp1)** ![★](https://img.shields.io/github/stars/crisprking/enpp1?style=flat-square&label=%E2%98%85&labelColor=1b1f23&color=e3b341) — structure-based paralog-selectivity counter-screen for the immuno-oncology target ENPP1 vs. ENPP2 (autotaxin) and ENPP3, ranked by cross-paralog margin.
- **[degradomap](https://github.com/crisprking/degradomap)** ![★](https://img.shields.io/github/stars/crisprking/degradomap?style=flat-square&label=%E2%98%85&labelColor=1b1f23&color=e3b341) — empirical evaluation of which public-data features actually predict PROTAC E3-ligase tractability.
- **[loeuf-safety-audit](https://github.com/crisprking/loeuf-safety-audit)** ![★](https://img.shields.io/github/stars/crisprking/loeuf-safety-audit?style=flat-square&label=%E2%98%85&labelColor=1b1f23&color=e3b341) — gnomAD LOEUF-based on-target safety audit: how much genetic constraint argues against knocking a target down.
